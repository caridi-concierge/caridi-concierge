## What Are Lip Fillers?
<b>Lip fillers</b> are injectable treatments that restore or add volume to the lips using hyaluronic acid (HA), a naturally occurring substance in your body that keeps skin hydrated and plump. ## Lip Flip vs Lip Filler: Which Is Right for You?
If you‚Äôve searched <b>‚Äúlip flip near me‚Äù</b>, you‚Äôve probably seen the buzz about this quick, non-filler alternative. A <b>lip flip</b> uses a small amount of Botox to relax the muscle above the upper lip, allowing it to gently roll upward. The result? A slightly fuller-looking top lip without adding volume.
<br />
A <b>lip flip</b> is a great, temporary way to <u>make your lips appear fuller</u>. It works by weakening the orbicularis oris muscle, the one you use for speaking or sipping through a straw. You might notice that those movements feel different for a few weeks, but the effect is temporary and usually very well tolerated.
<br />
<b>Lip filler</b>, on the other hand, <u>adds actual volume and structure using hyaluronic acid</u>. Some patients choose one or the other, but combining both can beautifully balance the lips, soft lift from Botox, structure from filler.

## Safety, Longevity, and Reversibility
Lip fillers are very safe when performed by a trained medical professional. All products used by Caridi Concierge are FDA-approved and fully reversible.
Lip fillers made of hyaluronic acid can be safely dissolved with an enzyme called hyaluronidase. You can remove lumps or migration while keeping the rest intact, or completely dissolve everything if needed. It gives patients control and peace of mind.
<br />
<b>Results typically last 6-12 months, depending on lifestyle and filler type.</b> Many patients choose maintenance treatments every 9 months to stay consistent without overfilling.

<br />

## How to Maintain Your Results
After your treatment, focus on hydration, sun protection, and gentle care. Drink water, use a nourishing lip balm, and avoid smoking, which can accelerate filler breakdown.
Touch-ups are optional but often done once or twice a year for best results. I encourage small, gradual enhancements over time. They look more natural and keep the lips healthy long-term.
